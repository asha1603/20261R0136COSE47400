Now I will explain our experiment and results.
-----------------------------------------------------------------
First, let me compare the structures of the two models.
On the left is our proposed model, U-Net CVAE,
and on the right is the baseline model, Vanilla CVAE.

Vanilla CVAE uses both the image and the label map as the encoder input.
Then, it uses the latent vector and the one-hot label in the decoder
to reconstruct the MRI image.

In contrast, U-Net CVAE only uses the image as the encoder input.
The label information is injected at the decoder bottleneck
through a label embedding.
Also, U-Net CVAE uses skip connections,
which directly pass encoder features to the decoder.
Finally, we added classifier-guided loss to the loss function.
This classifier loss encourages the reconstructed image
to be classified as the correct dementia stage.
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Next, this is the result comparison.
We compared MSE, PSNR, SSIM, reconstruction BCE, KL loss, and validation loss.
Overall, U-Net CVAE achieved better performance than Vanilla CVAE.

The difference is more clear when we look at the actual reconstruction results.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
First, this is the result of the baseline model, Vanilla CVAE.
Although images are generated for the three dementia stages,
they are generally blurry,
and many anatomical details are lost.

The confusion matrix on the right shows
which class the classifier predicted from the reconstructed images.
Here, 0 means Non-Demented,
1 means Very Mild Dementia,
and 2 means Mild Dementia.

In the Vanilla CVAE confusion matrix,
the classifier predicts most reconstruction images as Non-Demented.
This means that Vanilla CVAE does not preserve stage-specific features well.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Next, this is the result of U-Net CVAE.
Compared to Vanilla CVAE,
the reconstructed images are much sharper,
and the brain structure is better preserved.

In the confusion matrix,
the largest values appear along the diagonal.
The diagonal values mean that the true label
and the classifier prediction are the same.
Therefore, U-Net CVAE performs better than Vanilla CVAE
not only in image quality,
but also in dementia stage consistency.
